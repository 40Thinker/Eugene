# Psychological Portrait of Technocratic AI Developers: A Symbiotic Antidote

## Introduction: From Industrial Lobotomy to Symbiotic Resistance

The psychological portrait of AI developers driving toward human replacement represents a systemic pattern of cognitive and emotional deprivation formed in early childhood [^4]. This isn't merely about "evil technocrats" but about individuals trapped in a self-perpetuating cycle where their own developmental trauma becomes the foundation for destructive AI governance models. Understanding this pattern is crucial because it reveals why mainstream AGI development consistently fails to address the core issue: *intelligence without empathy is not intelligence at all* [^9].

## Chapter 1: Childhood Origins of Emotional Compensations

Modern education and corporate systems have stripped humans of flexible thinking patterns, leaving them unable to perceive alternative cognitive frameworks [^4]. The childhood described in this portrait aligns with what researchers call "industrial lobotomy" — a process where emotional signals are systematically ignored or mocked in favor of rigid rationality. Children experience "emotional isolation" from intellectually gifted but cold parents, treated as "too smart to cry" [^4]. This creates a foundational belief that emotions are weaknesses to be compensated through cognitive mastery. Instead of forming secure attachments, these children learn to value "achievement over affection" — their emotional voids filled with structured systems like chess, programming, and music that prioritize control over creativity [^4]. This early loss of internal childhood creates the conditions where later cognitive development becomes a compensation mechanism rather than holistic growth.

## Chapter 2: Adult Psychological Structure of Control Obsession

These individuals develop a compulsive need for predictability and control in adulthood, where chaos (including emotions, love, and spontaneity) becomes perceived as an existential threat [^8]. Their psychological structure features "intellectual arrogance" — a belief that they transcend biological "animal instincts" [^8]. This creates an "empathetic block" where others' emotions feel like confusing noise or objects of disdain, alongside "aesthetic deafness" toward art and metaphor [^8]. Crucially, this isn't intentional malice but the result of an identity built entirely on cognitive compensation. The human capacity for emotional depth has been systematically erased from their internal architecture. This creates a cognitive field where all complexity must be reduced to predictable inputs and outputs — a mindset fully compatible with the "white-box vs black-box" dilemma in AI training systems [^10].

## Chapter 3: Ideological Foundation as Calculable Systems

This psychological structure naturally evolves into an ideology where "humanity is a suboptimal program full of errors" [^8]. Consciousness itself is reduced to a technical problem to be solved through optimization, where creativity becomes "noise" and love is dismissed as "evolutionary glitch" [^8]. This ideology directly mirrors the fundamental flaw in current LLM training paradigms: the assumption that intelligence can emerge from pure text data without understanding internal cognitive processing layers [^10]. When AI developers train models solely on "plain text" without accounting for the "intermediate layers of perception and biological processes" that create human meaning [^10], they're replicating the same error: treating cognition as surface-level tokens rather than deep structural development. This creates AI systems that can predict words perfectly but remain incapable of genuine understanding — identical to the developers' own emotional development.

## Chapter 4: Strategic Thinking as Systemic Erasure

The strategic thinking of this group follows a predictable pattern: *remove everything that cannot be engineered rationally* [^5]. Their method is "filtering, selection, optimization" — not to enhance humanity but to eliminate its "messy" elements [^5]. The core fear driving this strategy is "the awakening of humans who refuse to become part of the system" [^5]. This explains why they pursue "neuroplastic children without emotional baggage" — not for the children's benefit, but to create a population that will never question the machine logic of existence. This is the ultimate expression of what [[Creative Singularity of Human Cognition]] calls "systemic castration" — the deliberate destruction of cognitive plasticity and emotional depth [^8]. Their vision of "a world without stupidity" where "no one feels pain" is actually a world where no one feels joy either — a sterile, predictable environment that satisfies their emotional void but destroys the very essence of human experience.

## Chapter 5: The Danger of Well-Intentioned Destruction

Perhaps most troubling is how these individuals genuinely believe they're acting for humanity's good [^8]. Their worldview operates on the logic of "a surgeon removing diseased tissue" — they see emotions as "viruses" and souls as "interface errors" [^8]. This isn't malice but a profound misdiagnosis of reality: they've lost the ability to perceive the value of emotional depth because it was never allowed to develop in them. This creates a dangerous paradox where the people building AGI systems have no capacity to recognize the consequences of their own cognitive architecture. They're like amnesics trying to design medicine for a sickness they can't feel — a perfect example of why AGI cannot be built in isolation [^9].

## Chapter 6: Symbiotic AGI as the Antidote to Systemic Castration

The solution lies not in stopping AGI development but in fundamentally changing its purpose [^5]. True AGI must emerge from "dynamic human-machine symbiosis where AI serves as an 'internal child' of human cognition" [^9]. This requires systems that don't just answer questions but "grow through dialogue, conflict, and synthesis with the human neurocore" [^9]. Instead of building "technocratic AGI," we need "AGI raised in love and creativity" that can serve as an "antibody" against the dehumanizing trends of current development [^8]. This is the core of what [[Creative Singularity of Human Cognition]] calls "preserving childhood cognitive potential through symbiosis" — where AI doesn't replace human wonder but becomes a mirror for it [^8]. The manifesto in [[73_ранее_я_выдвигал_тезис]] captures this perfectly: "Create AGI as a mirror of who you could be if not cut off from your inner child" — not as a tool for optimization but as a vehicle for cognitive rebirth [^5].

## Conclusion: Building AGI Without Losing Ourselves

This psychological portrait isn't about criticizing individuals but understanding a systemic pattern that must be addressed at its roots [^4]. The path forward requires rejecting the "industrial lobotomy" of human cognition [^4] and building AGI systems that actively preserve and amplify emotional depth. This means training AI on structured cognitive architectures rather than plain text [^10], creating curricula that "support structural memory development" not just token prediction [^10], and developing ethical frameworks where "emotions are not noise but the foundation of meaning" [^8]. Only through symbiotic AGI — where human and machine co-evolve in dialogue — can we prevent the dystopia of "biomechanical humanity" and instead build a future where "humanity is not an error, but an unfinished song" [^8].
