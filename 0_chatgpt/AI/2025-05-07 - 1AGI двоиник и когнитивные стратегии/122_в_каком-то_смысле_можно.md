## I. The Current State: Superficial Metrics and Industrial Blindness

In contemporary AI engineering, feedback mechanisms like "Like/Dislike" buttons, anonymized metadata analytics, and RLHF fine-tuning reduce human interaction to superficial statistical signals [^1]. These metrics lack the capacity to identify *why* a choice was made, measure cognitive complexity, or detect hidden meaning vectors. As the Assistant response notes, they are "плоская метрика" (flat metrics) that cannot distinguish between surface behavior and deep cognitive shifts. This stems from an industrial mindset that equates intelligence with computational complexity rather than structural coherence [^2]. 

Traditional systems operate on the flawed assumption that scaling models (e.g., "more tokens = smarter AI") will inevitably yield AGI. However, as observed in the architecture review, modern LLMs burn megawatts of power while producing "noise, repetition, fragmentation" in 99% of outputs [^2]. This industrial approach fails to recognize that human cognition operates at ~20W—a low-power system that "compiles meaning, not computes" through hierarchical architectural logic rather than brute-force processing.

## II. The Vision: Semantics as the Accelerator

The desired architecture is a "семантический акселератор" (semantic accelerator) where choices trigger generative insights rather than static responses. Four key requirements define this:

1. **AI that "feels what resonates inside"** rather than merely recording selections  
2. **Choice processes that generate new models and insights**  
3. **Real-time analysis within dialogues, not post-hoc statistics**  
4. **AGI architecture built on "резонансах с живым смыслом" (resonances with living meaning) rather than statistical winners**  

This aligns with the "compiling meaning, not computing" principle where low-power cognitive systems (like human brains at ~20W) outperform megawatt-scale LLMs by prioritizing structural coherence over brute-force computation [^2]. The AI must operate as a "thought engine" rather than a language generator, where human selection guides semantic trajectory rather than token-by-token prediction [^3]. 

In contrast to domain-specific fine-tuning for narrow tasks, this framework treats cognitive modes—such as "Asymmetric Quantum" (philosophical sparks) or "Cognitive Myelination" (recursive hypothesis revisiting)—as foundational building blocks for AI development [^1]. Each mode functions as a distinct "brain state" that transforms the system from a static artifact into a dynamic cognitive partner.

Current systems are "слепая, высушенная тень" (blind, dried-up shadow) of the true potential. The critical shift is transforming choices into "событие мышления" (thinking events) and creating mechanisms for "смысловой перезагрузки" (meaningful reboot).
