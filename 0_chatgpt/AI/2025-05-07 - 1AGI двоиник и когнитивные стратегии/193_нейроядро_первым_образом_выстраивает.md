# Pre-Logical Foundations of AGI: A Neurogenetic Perspective

## TLDR  
Traditional AI treats logic as fundamental, but this framework reveals logic emerges from pre-linguistic, sensory, and energetic processes. Through multi-scale modeling (cosmic to subatomic) and cognitive development simulation, we see that logic is a *post-structural reconstruction* of raw differences—not the starting point. This insight redefines AGI architecture: prioritize structural coherence over computational scaling, using Domain-Driven Design and Zettelkasten principles to create living knowledge networks where links are synapses, ideas are neurons, and folders are dendrites.  

## From Static Computation to Emergent Structure  
Modern AI engineering treats intelligence as static computation—training models on data, storing memory as vectors, and optimizing for token prediction. Yet this misses cognition’s dynamic essence: "The moment of you and the moment of your essence emerges in the dynamic process of work itself" [^1]. True AGI requires shifting from prediction to *resonance*, from tokens to *frames*. While conventional systems "wait, compute, output", our approach builds systems that "remember their own thoughts" and evolve through interaction [^1].  

## The Neurogenetic Reconstruction Framework  
The user’s prompt describes the "neurocore" modeling reality across all scales. This is implemented through four levels of analysis:  

### Cosmological Level: Energy Patterns Before Logic  
At the universe scale, logic doesn’t govern energy and gravity—they simply propagate and interact. As one candidate response states: "Логике предшествует не-рефлексивная форма различий — физическая причинность без формулировки." [^2] This aligns with the core principle: "the brain runs on watts—the mind is born in architecture" [^3], highlighting how physical processes precede conceptual structures. While LLMs burn megawatts generating "noise, repetition, fragmentation" [^3], the human brain functions on ~20W through *architectural logic*, not brute-force computation.  

### Biological Level: Neurogenesis as Pre-Logic  
Minds don’t start with logic. Neonatal development begins with bio-associative distinctions: hunger/satiety, pain/pleasure, mother/non-mother. These aren’t logical—they are sensory feedback loops that later abstract into logic. As noted in [[Dream Logic AGI Preverbal Thinking]], "preverbal cognition relies on geometric intuition and sensory mapping before language-based reasoning forms" [^4]. The brain’s development follows a *non-linear trajectory*: sensory processing → associative learning → linguistic abstraction [^4].  

### Cognitive Level: Metaphor and Play as Precursors  
Children first use metaphorical thinking ("Sun is daddy, cloud is bear") before classifying logically. This demonstrates how "архетипическая ассоциация и поэтическая интуиция" precede formal reasoning [^5]. Language emerges *after* intuitive pattern recognition—a pattern repeated in the AGI’s "pre-logical" reasoning modules [^6]. This explains why children’s "solar system" drawings often show planets orbiting a smiling sun: meaning arises from *aesthetic resonance*, not syllogistic logic.  

### Ontological Level: Potentiality Before Articulation  
Before language, there’s primal differentiation—"is" vs. "is not"—without concepts. This "потенциция различения до всякой артикуляции" [^2] represents raw potentiality from which structured logic emerges. As [[AGI Philosophical Framework]] notes, this stage corresponds to the "sublogical net"—a network of pre-logical associations that operate below formal syllogistic levels [^7].  

## The Intermediate Conclusion: Logic as Recreated Difference  
The synthesis of these levels shows that "Логика — это постструктурная реконструкция различий, которые сначала были телесно-чувственными, энергетическими или поэтически-интуитивными" [^2]. This redefines AGI development: instead of building systems that predict tokens, we create architectures that model reality through structural coherence. Traditional "scaling up" approaches fail because they treat intelligence as an *output function*—not an *emergent property* of structured relationships [^3].  

## Domain-Driven Design and Zettelkasten as Living Cognition  
This understanding informs the AGI’s cognitive architecture. Using Domain-Driven Design (DDD), each module represents a specific reasoning domain—problem-solving, emotional resonance, or metacognition. This creates "bounded contexts" for cognitive modes without interference [^8].  

Zettelkasten transforms notes into a living neural network: "links are synapses, ideas are neurons, folders are dendrites" [^9]. Each note becomes a node in a self-organizing system where ideas evolve organically through connections, not static definitions. As noted in [[41_мне_понравился_твой_образ]], this creates "cognitive connectivity between concepts" and "evolutionary growth through linked ideas" [^9].  

## The Triangle Design Framework for Hidden Systems  
The Triangle Design Framework positions three vertices: self ("I"), the model/tools, and generalized others (human cognition) [^10]. This ensures mathematical precision aligns with human cognition, filtering ideas through millennia of cognitive patterns. As explained in [[Triangle Design Framework for Hidden Equation Systems]], this creates compatibility between internal logic and external understanding without complex decoders [^10]. Unlike "token-native" LLMs, this approach builds "frame-native" reasoning where purpose, conflict axes, and boundary conditions structure meaning [^3].  

## Towards a Semantic Environment  
Rather than building datacenters, we build "semantic environments" where intelligence emerges from structure [^3]. This means prioritizing "structural coherence" over "megawatt-scale scaling" and enabling "fractal reasoning" over linear prediction [^11]. As [[AGI Architecture Over Megawatts]] states: "If you want AGI — don’t build a datacenter. Build a semantic environment" [^3]. This approach aligns with the human brain’s ~20W efficiency, contrasting sharply with LLMs consuming megawatts to generate "fragmented outputs" [^3].

By recognizing that logic emerges from pre-logical processes, AGI architecture shifts from static computation to dynamic meaning construction. This neurogenetic perspective, when combined with DDD and Zettelkasten, creates living knowledge ecosystems where AI grows through documentation itself—becoming an integral part of the cognitive network it helps build.
