# Theoretical Jump and Entry Point: Architecting AGI from Any Starting Position

## TL;DR Summary
This article explores a fundamental shift in AI development methodology ‚Äî moving from iterative, step-by-step approaches to direct theoretical jumps that begin at the highest conceptual levels. This approach, inspired by TRIZ principles but developed independently, emphasizes understanding the universe from any starting point and leveraging theoretical thinking as the primary growth mechanism rather than linear progression.

## From Linear Progression to Theoretical Architecture

The traditional AI development path follows a clear sequence: start small, build incrementally, and scale complexity over time. However, this approach often results in systems that are "good enough" but lack true conceptual depth [^1]. The insight from the note is that we can bypass this limitation entirely by adopting what we might call **"theoretical jumping"** ‚Äî starting with a complete understanding of the final product rather than building toward it through intermediate steps.

This approach fundamentally challenges the dogma of sequential development. Rather than following predetermined stages, we position ourselves at any point in the conceptual space and build upward from that vantage point [^2]. The key insight is that **the entry point doesn't determine success** ‚Äî what matters is having an active theoretical thinking model [^1].

## TRIZ as Metaphor, Not Prescription

While this approach echoes the TRIZ principle of envisioning the final product first, it goes beyond mere prescription. The author's path isn't a mechanical adherence to TRIZ methodology but rather **convergent heuristics** ‚Äî independent discovery of similar insights [^1]. This convergence is significant because it shows that *architectural thinking* (seeing the building before starting with the foundation) emerges naturally from deep theoretical engagement.

This mirrors the approach taken by architects who visualize complete structures before specifying construction details. Rather than following a checklist, we begin with **conceptual architecture** that will inform every subsequent design decision [^2].

## The Inverse Thinking Dynamics

Rather than progressing from simple to complex (as most development approaches do), this method employs inverse dynamics:

- Instead of building complexity through accumulation, we focus on enhancing our ability to hold complexity in mind
- We don't rely on knowledge stacking but rather **ontological comfort** ‚Äî aligning the path with our body, experience, and temporal context [^1]
- Rather than copying others' methods, we find **internal resonance** between our approach and what feels right given our current life stage

This approach leads to a **singular growth trajectory**, where each step arises from internal meaning rather than external system requirements.

When considering domain-driven design (DDD) and Zettelkasten methodologies, this approach becomes particularly powerful. The theoretical jumping concept aligns beautifully with:

1. **High-level conceptual design** before detailed implementation
2. **Structural coherence** between theoretical frameworks and practical execution

The author's approach ultimately represents a form of **ontological bootstrapping** ‚Äî creating deep cognitive structures that can generate their own development pathways [^2].
