## üîÑ Traditional AI Engineering: The Empirical Path

The mainstream AI engineer begins with a clear, operational goal: "I want to train my model." Their entire workflow is structured around **measurable outputs**: loss curves, token accuracy, inference speed, and pull requests merged[^1]. The model is treated as a computational black box whose internal states are relevant only insofar as they correlate with performance metrics.

- **Tools**: Colab, Hugging Face, LoRA, fine-tuning scripts
- **Focus**: weights, gradients, attention heatmaps
- **Success criteria**: higher accuracy, faster inference, better alignment

This approach is empirically grounded: "If it works, it's good." But it hits a ceiling when asked: *"Why does it work?"* or *"How could it think?"* At that point, most engineers retreat into pragmatism‚Äîmore data, better prompts, stronger regularization‚Äîbecause the architecture of thought lies outside the scope of their training and reward systems[^1].
